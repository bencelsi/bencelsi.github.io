John Adams Poem

Oh, you've heard of Thomas Jefferson George Washington as well. But not of poor John Adams, So his story I will tell: He was born to hardworking family Who wanted him to go to school. But he didn't care what people said, So he broke their studious rules. As he slacked along, he realized That education was propellin' He decided he would go to school. But no one had to tell 'im. He went to Harvard College And a lawyer he became. His rebellious personality Would lead him to his fame. He pushed for independence, Which left him quite forespent. By 1797, though, He was the president. "Oh, go to war with France," Alex Hamilton implored him. But Adams was a stubborn man, So likewise, he ignored him. Because he wouldn't go to war, His term was of the dullest. He became very unpopular Among the Federalists. Yet, despite these minor details, He was honest, true, and wise. People saw in him the bitter truth Though he meant to tell them lies. It wasn't that surprising That he lost for re-election, For his reign was catastrophic, While Jefferson's was perfection. He stomped out of the office, His life a tangled mess, He sat, his face screwed up in hate, Quite thoroughly depressed.
